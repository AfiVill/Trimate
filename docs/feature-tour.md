# Trimate — Feature Tour

A guided walk through the Trimate workspace: what each surface is for and how a real trip flows through them. For the system design behind these features, see the [architecture overview](architecture.md).

## The sidebar: trip controls

Everything in Trimate is driven by the trip configuration in the sidebar:

- **Destination** — chosen from the curated catalog of destinations, regions, and activity anchors.
- **Passport country** — drives the entry-requirement and visa logic.
- **Travel month** — feeds seasonality and weather signals.
- **Trip style** — Adventure, Culture, Food, Nature, Beach, or Mixed; biases which anchors the route planner selects and how days are themed.
- **Budget level** — Low, Medium, or High; scales the daily allowances used by the budget engine.
- **Risk tolerance** — Low, Medium, or High; scales the contingency buffer.
- **Pacing mode** — Slow, Balanced, or Packed; governs anchors-per-day and recovery buffers in the itinerary.
- **Hotel filters** — star rating, nightly-rate bounds, and cancellation-policy filters applied to the hotel board.

Change any control and the entire workspace — insights, route, budget — reacts immediately.

## 1 · Dashboard

The command center. It surfaces **high-level insights** generated by the insight engine (seasonality, safety posture, price findings from your logs), a **destination explorer** for browsing the curated catalog by style and vibe, and a **route map preview** of the current itinerary.

*Use it to:* sanity-check the trip at a glance before and during research.

## 2 · Flights reviewed

The airfare observation log. Capture each fare you encounter — origin, destination, airline, duration, price — and the **flight analytics** panel aggregates your log into median, average, and best-available figures per route.

*Use it to:* stop losing fares you saw yesterday, and know instantly whether today's price is actually good.

## 3 · Hotels reviewed

The lodging shortlist, structured. Log candidate properties with name, area, star rating, cancellation policy, and nightly rate. The **filtered hotel board** applies your sidebar filters so only properties matching your standards remain in view, alongside rate analytics across your log.

*Use it to:* compare a scattered hotel shortlist on equal, filterable terms.

## 4 · Entry + safety

Passport-aware trip clearance. The **entry logic** panel resolves entry requirements for your passport/destination pair from the catalog's knowledge base, while **safety + weather** presents advisory posture and seasonal weather signals for the selected travel month.

*Use it to:* catch visa and safety blockers early — before money is committed.

## 5 · Route planner

The itinerary engine. **Planner controls** let you tune the generation, and the **full route table** lays out the day-by-day plan: anchors per day themed to your trip style, segment distances computed via haversine great-circle modeling, and realistic transfer-time estimates between stops. Pacing mode decides how dense each day is and how much recovery buffer it keeps.

*Use it to:* turn a destination and a style into a concrete, honest day-by-day plan.

## 6 · Budget engine

The financial rollup. Compiles three explicit components — your **observed market costs** (from the flight and hotel logs), **tiered daily allowances** (food, local transport, activities — scaled by budget level), and a **contingency buffer** (scaled by risk tolerance) — into one master trip budget with every line traceable to its source.

*Use it to:* replace the spreadsheet that never adds up with a budget you can defend.

## 7 · Collaboration + reports

The exit ramp. Generate a polished **PDF trip brief** of the entire plan, exchange data via **CSV export/import** or direct **database handoff**, and manage **saved trip sessions** so any planning state can be restored later.

*Use it to:* hand a finished, professional plan to travel companions — no app required on their end.

## A typical planning session

1. **Configure** the trip in the sidebar: destination, passport, month, style, pacing.
2. **Check clearance** in *Entry + safety* — confirm the trip is viable before investing time.
3. **Research and log** fares and properties into *Flights reviewed* and *Hotels reviewed* as you browse.
4. **Watch the Dashboard** as insights sharpen with every observation you add.
5. **Generate the route** in *Route planner* and tune pacing until the days feel right.
6. **Review the rollup** in *Budget engine* — adjust budget level or risk tolerance and watch the numbers respond.
7. **Export** the PDF brief and save the session in *Collaboration + reports*.
